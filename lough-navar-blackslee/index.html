Route

If you’re thinking of going to Blackslee waterfall in Lough Navar, this info might help.

Park at the car park:

Note: to the left of the cars, there’s a trail up to the seat you can make out above the cars.

For Blackslee, walk downhill towards the right of the picture – follow the signpost at the bottom.

Follow the trail – stay right (that is, any time there’s a choice of direction, go right). Some work has been done on the trail (tree-felling I think), so to allow the lorries in, the first part has been resurfaced. This is what the surface looks like:

As you can see, the gravel is a bit large and makes walking a little awkward – perhaps especially for a pregnant lady. Walking in the tyre track makes it easier.

We’re staying right – remember?

Holding the camera level to try and give an idea of gradient – it’s about 70 steps to cover the bottom of this hill to the top (I counted):

Stay right (the post to the right points to the car-park):

Staying right means going around the back of the picnic table – the gate is almost always closed – but if it’s open, you don’t go through it:

Go to the left or right of the picnic table – your choice!

Just before the waterfall (you will be able to hear the waterfall), there’s a little place to stop off):

This is the steepest part – the steps to the waterfall. Covered in moss/pine needles, so in the wet be careful:

You made it!

Back at the car park, this is the view from the picnic table you can see above the cars (look back at the first picture):

Back to the car, drive past the trail to Blackslee and continue for 200/300 metres. You’ll see a sign for the Lough Slawn trail

Across the road for the Lough Slawn trail, there’s another hidden trail – can you see it (don’t go up it as it’s always very wet)

Back on the Lough Slawn Trail (in this picture, the road is 10 meters behind us), you’re walking up this path and into the trees on the right. The trail ends on top of the escarpment you see on the left:

Sort of in-the-middle of the last picture, some 300 million year old sandstone – the post on the right has some information about it:

Two trails for the price of one – try both (they’re only 100 meters long and there’s a seat at the end of both):

Lough Slawn (really just a big pond). And a really bad picture.

And from the viewpoint (note: I’m taking the picture of the road you’re parked on and I’m waaaay closer to the edge than you’re going to get):
